Common questions before you buy

What if my customers don't reply to the texts?+
Industry data shows 20-40% of customers respond to personalised service reminders. Even at the low end, that's dozens of recovered jobs per year. The texts aren't generic blasts — they reference the customer's address, pest history, and when they're due. That personal touch is why the response rate is so high.
I'm not tech-savvy. Is this hard to set up?+
If you can send a text message, you can use PestFlow. Setup takes about 5 minutes — type in your customers or paste from a spreadsheet. Or add our Done-For-You setup ($149 one-time) and we'll do everything for you in 24 hours. Just send us your customer list in any format.
Can I cancel anytime?+
Yes. Monthly plans have zero contracts — cancel from your dashboard with one click, anytime. No cancellation fees, no questions asked. Lifetime deal customers never need to worry about this — you pay once and it's yours forever.
How is this different from just texting customers myself?+
You could text them yourself — if you remembered to, every time, for every customer. PestFlow watches your entire list and automatically flags who's overdue. Then it uses AI to write a personalised message that references their specific service, property, and pest history. It does in 10 seconds what would take you 20 minutes of scrolling through your records.
What if I already use ServiceTitan / FieldRoutes / Jobber?+
PestFlow doesn't replace your field management software. It does one thing those platforms do poorly: proactive customer retention. Think of it as a specialist that plugs the leak in your customer bucket. You can export your customer list from any tool and import it into PestFlow in minutes.
$29/month seems cheap. Is this actually good?+
It's cheap on purpose. We built PestFlow for independent operators, not enterprise companies with $300/month software budgets. The price is low because our costs are low — we use AI to do the heavy lifting. One recovered customer ($150+ job) pays for 5 months of the starter plan.
